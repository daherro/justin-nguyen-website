The title of the essay, set with room to breathe

A standfirst paragraph that frames the piece — the question it sets out to answer, or the claim it is going to make.

This is the opening paragraph of the post. Write the way you would explain the idea to a sharp friend who has no context — plainly, in full sentences, building the argument one step at a time. The drop capital sets the start of the piece.

A second paragraph continues the thought. Keep the measure narrow and the line height generous so long passages stay comfortable to read. Links inside the text look like this underlined gold, and emphasis can be carried with italics or the occasional bolder weight.

A section heading

Sections break a longer piece into readable movements. Each one can hold a few paragraphs of argument or narrative before the next turn in the thinking.

A pulled quote sits in Cormorant italic and celadon — the line you most want a skimming reader to remember.

After the quote, the argument resumes. You can also drop in a list when you need to enumerate:

  • A first point, stated cleanly.
  • A second point that builds on it.
  • A third that lands the idea.

A closing section

End where the thinking ends — no need to over-summarise. A final paragraph that leaves the reader with the one idea you most want them to carry away.
